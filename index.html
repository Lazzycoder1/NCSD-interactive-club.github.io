About E-waste

Any electronic product or accessory which has reached the end of its useful life is called E-waste. For example, discarded or obsolete Air Conditioners, TV, Washing Machine, Refrigerator, Fluorescent and other Mercury-containing lamps, etc.

Do's

  1. Follow the instructions for proper handling of the end of life equipment in product user manual.
  2. Protect the glass surface of any electronic product to avoid breakage.
  3. Always drop your used electronic products to Authorized E-waste Collection Centers
Don'ts
  1. Do not dismantle your electronic products on your own.
  2. Do not throw electronics in bins having Do not Dispose.
  3. Do not give e-waste to informal (Kabbadi) and unorganized sectors like Local Scrap Dealer Rag Pickers.

Aim

The main aim of the project is to increase awareness and share information about latest e-wase recyling methods instead of losing the resource to waste disposal or incineration plants and collection outside official take-back systems. To achieve that, the project focuses on education and involvement of school children, both to raise the awareness of end-of-life electronics as a resource, and through them to bring the message into families and the society as whole.

Types of Recyling

  • Data destruction

    Securty of the confidential data stored in the hard drives of your PC's, Laptops and servers is a concern. The data is completely destroyed

  • Certified Recycling

    Many E-waste recycling companies provide a 'Green Certificate' or a 'Destruction Certificate' to each of their clients after the safe disposal of their e-waste

  • IT Refurbish

    Repair of used electronics assets to bring them back to life ,is the least polluting process of resposible recycling
